# Title Officer & Title Unit Self-Evaluation Form

## Questions Reference

---

## Header Information (Required)

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Company | Company name |
| Title Officer / Unit Leader | Name of the person completing the evaluation |
| Title Unit / Location | Title unit or office location |
| Evaluation Period | Time period being evaluated (e.g., Q4 2025, Jan-Dec 2025) |
| Date Completed | Date the evaluation was completed |

---

## Section I – Technical Title Competency

### 1. Title Knowledge & Expertise

**Instructions:** Describe your current level of proficiency in title examination, underwriting guidelines, curative practices, and jurisdiction-specific issues. Identify areas of strength and areas requiring improvement. Include steps taken within the last year to enhance your technical knowledge.

---

### 2. Risk Identification & Problem Resolution

**Instructions:** Evaluate your effectiveness in identifying title defects, managing complex title issues, and resolving problems prior to closing. Cite examples of challenges, errors, or near-misses and what was learned from them.

---

## Section II – Operational Performance

### 3. Workload Management & Turnaround Times

**Instructions:** Assess how effectively your unit manages file volume, turnaround expectations, and priority transactions while maintaining accuracy. Identify recurring bottlenecks or inefficiencies.

---

### 4. Process Consistency & Quality Control

**Instructions:** Describe the systems, checks, or workflows in place to ensure consistent quality and minimize errors. Identify gaps or areas where controls could be strengthened.

---

## Section III – Customer Interaction & Communication

### 5. Customer Service & Client Experience

**Instructions:** Objectively evaluate your unit's responsiveness, professionalism, and clarity when interacting with customers, lenders, attorneys, and agents. Reference client feedback, trends, or service metrics where available.

---

### 6. Internal & External Communication

**Instructions:** Assess the effectiveness of communication within your unit and with other departments (escrow/closing, sales, underwriting). Identify instances where communication breakdowns affected outcomes.

---

## Section IV – Leadership & Management (If Applicable)

### 7. Team Leadership & Accountability

**Instructions:** Evaluate your leadership approach, including delegation, accountability, and performance management. How do you address underperformance or behavioral issues?

---

### 8. Training, Cross-Training & Succession Risk

**Instructions:** Describe how your unit trains new staff, develops existing team members, and mitigates key-person dependency risk.

---

## Section V – Compliance & Judgment

### 9. Underwriting, Compliance & Decision-Making

**Instructions:** Assess your adherence to underwriting requirements, regulatory obligations, and company policies. Provide examples of high-risk or time-sensitive decisions and their outcomes.

---

## Section VI – Overall Assessment

### 10. Overall Unit Evaluation & Improvement Plan

**Instructions:** If evaluating your title unit as an underwriter or external auditor, identify:
- Core strengths
- Primary risks or vulnerabilities
- Top three operational or strategic improvements you would implement immediately

---

## Executive Scoring Rubric (Management Use Only)

| Score | Rating | Description |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| 5 | Exceptional | Consistently exceeds expectations; best-in-class performance; minimal risk exposure |
| 4 | Strong | Frequently exceeds expectations; low risk; well-controlled operations |
| 3 | Satisfactory | Meets expectations; manageable risk; improvement opportunities identified |
| 2 | Needs Improvement | Inconsistent performance; elevated risk; corrective action required |
| 1 | Deficient | Fails to meet expectations; material risk exposure; immediate intervention required |

### Scoring Categories

Management can score each section:
- Technical Competency (Section I)
- Operational Performance (Section II)
- Customer Communication (Section III)
- Leadership (Section IV)
- Compliance (Section V)
- Overall (Section VI)
